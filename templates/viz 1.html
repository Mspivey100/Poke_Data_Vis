Max Temperature

The data indicate a relationship between latitude and temperature, where cities closest to the equator have the highest temperatures, and cities furthest from the equator have the coldest temperatures. The differences between the coldest and warmest maximum temperatures is greater for the Northern hemisphere than the Southern hemisphere. The hottest temperatures are found at latitudes of 20-40 degrees, peaking at 40 degrees North. The coldest temperatures are found at latitudes of 60-80 degrees North.
